> Source: **"(For external use) KOICA Evaluation Quality-Review Guidelines v2" (2025.6, KOICA Evaluation Office)** — Table 2, Table 3, results table (p.1-8). Supplementary: "Guidelines for Conducting Evaluation" (2024).
> **This is not "project evaluation" (grades A–F) but "quality inspection of the evaluation report" (grades A–D).** It looks not at *whether the project went well* but at *whether the evaluation report was well written* (meta-evaluation). The `report-quality-inspector` rubric.

---

## Scoring Structure (core)

- **24 questions grouped into 10 detailed items** for scoring. Each detailed item: **Excellent 10 / Good 8 / Somewhat poor 6 / Poor 4**.
- 10 × 10 = **100 points maximum**. Section allocation: Ⅰ 20 · Ⅱ 20 · Ⅲ 30 · Ⅳ 20 · Ⅴ 10 (v2 Table 2, totals consistent).
- Final score = **the average of the composite scores of the 3 panel members**. The appropriateness of the total score and final grade must be checked separately (v2 p.3).

## Table 3 — Final Quality Grade  ★ v2: Pass floor is **60 points**

| Grade | Score range | Result | Qualitative criteria (newly established in v2, p.3-4) |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|---|
| **A Excellent** | 90 or higher | Pass | Theory-of-Change analysis systematic + evidence-based lessons/feedback tasks are concrete and actionable |
| **B Good** | 80 to below 90 | Pass | Lessons and recommendations logically connected, with concrete improvement measures presented |
| **C Fair** | **60 to below 80** | Pass | Lessons and recommendations somewhat abstract, with limited feasibility |
| **D Poor** | **Below 60** | **Non-Pass** | Lessons and recommendations presented without logical grounds, or including infeasible ones |

> ⚠️ **The v2 official Non-Pass boundary is 60 points.** (The 70 points in the older extract is inconsistent with v2 → corrected to 60. Reports scoring 60–70 are not failures but C (Pass).)
> **Follow-up for a D grade (v2 p.2)**: the evaluation team supplements the report reflecting the panel-review results → quality **re-review** → the secretariat (Evaluation Office) delivers the final result to the department under evaluation.
> (※ Items such as 6-month notification, one round / 1-month supplementation, invalidation/deletion of the project evaluation grade upon a confirmed D, public disclosure, A grade = excellent evaluator, panel term of 2 years, etc. are **not explicitly stated in the v2 body text** — they are based on the Project Evaluation Regulation / Guidelines for Conducting Evaluation.)

## Table 2 — 10 Detailed Items / 24 Questions (v2 official names and mapping)

### Ⅰ. Evaluation design and methodology (20 = 2)
**① Appropriateness of the evaluation background and context analysis (10)** — Questions 1·2
1. Was the project under evaluation sufficiently explained (is the project overview clear, and do the region, schedule, budget, implementation status, beneficiary needs and context, stakeholders, partnerships, and contribution come through)?
2. Is the design rooted in a PDM or Theory of Change (including causality between output and outcome and the review/reconstruction of the PDM)?

**② Rigor of the evaluation design and methodology (10)** — Questions 3–6
3. Were the evaluation criteria and questions suited to the nature of the given project selected?
4. Does the evaluation matrix have measurement indicators, data sources, analysis methods, and even sampling in place?
5. (Where relevant to a policy marker) Is it a design/methodology that properly reflects cross-cutting issues (gender equality, human rights, environment)?
6. Did it disclose the limitations of the methodology itself and address their effects and biases and how they would be overcome?

### Ⅱ. Reliability of data (20 = 2)  ※ v2 mapping (Questions 7-8 / Questions 9-10)
**③ Validity of the data-collection process and data quality (10)** — Questions 7·8
7. Did it properly disclose how the data were collected, analyzed, and sampled?
8. Did it use objective, reliable, and valid data and clearly state their sources?

**④ Appropriateness of data quality and verification (10)** — Questions 9·10
9. Were the data collected via a human-rights-based approach (HRBA), attending to ethical sensitivities?
10. Were qualitative and quantitative data appropriately cross-verified (including cross-verification of the degree of results achievement)?

### Ⅲ. Analysis and evaluation results (30 = 3)  ※ v2 mapping (Question 11 / Questions 12-13 / Questions 14-15)
**⑤ Appropriateness of the analysis method and reliability of the evaluation results (10)** — Question 11
11. Were quantitative and qualitative analyses performed accurately to derive the evaluation results?

**⑥ Logicality of the derivation of results (10)** — Questions 12·13
12. Did it provide concrete grounds to support the evaluation results (meaning facts and data, excluding personal statements, hearsay, and opinions)?
13. Was the "degree of project-results achievement" re-confirmed by several objective and reliable means?

**⑦ Validity of the evidence-based interpretation (10)** — Questions 14·15
14. Was every evaluation question raised given an analysis and an answer?
15. Did it identify and describe the causal factors (situational, organizational, managerial) that determined whether the logic model worked or not?

### Ⅳ. Conclusions and recommendations (20 = 2)
**⑧ Validity of the evaluation conclusions (10)** — Questions 16–18
16. Were the conclusions derived without bias, as a natural consequence of the evaluation results?
17. Are the conclusions ones that do not stop at merely summarizing the results but bring full understanding of the underlying issues?
18. **Does the finally calculated evaluation grade match the evaluation content in the body text?** *(The Cambodia report's composite-score mismatch of "Korean 11.7 vs English 12.7" is caught by exactly this question.)*

**⑨ Feasibility and concreteness of the recommendations (10)** — Questions 19–20
19. Were the lessons and feedback tasks drawn out logically and clearly, without overlapping one another?
20. Are the feedback tasks **SMART** and action-oriented, realistic and concrete?

### Ⅴ. Report structure and format (10 = 1)
**⑩ Clarity of the report summary and structure (10)** — Questions 21–24
21. Is the summary, read on its own, both concise and sufficient?
22. Is the composition logical and the length appropriate (body within 40 pages)?
23. Is it free of grammar, typo, and punctuation errors so that it reads easily?
24. Are the Korean/English summary, field-survey overview, daily activities, list of interviews, questionnaire, and references attached?

## Quality-Review Overall Assessment (newly established in v2, p.8)
**At least 200 characters** (excluding whitespace and spacing). 4 required items:
1. Major strengths
2. Point-deduction factors / items needing improvement
3. Points to note for future evaluations
4. Proposals to enhance the usability of the evaluation results

## Review Method (v2)
- **Evaluation Quality Review Committee**: chair + 3 panel members + secretariat (Evaluation Office) = 5 persons. Panel = 1–2 evaluation experts + 1–2 field-specific experts (3 total).
- Principle of **blind** review (the evaluator's information is not disclosed). **Recusal**: participants in the target project's preliminary survey, planning, implementation plan, implementation, or Final Evaluation, and those lacking impartiality (p.4).
- **Written review + convened meeting (video conference) in parallel** → feedback and follow-up (p.2, procedure ①②③).
- **Required documents**: evaluation results report · evaluation-grade results table · evaluation brief (Korean and English). Related: project overview, implementation plan, interim-review report, implementing-agency report.
- Composite score per panel member → average → grade (A–D) and Pass/Non-Pass determination.

## Consolidated Grade Calculation Table for Evaluation Services (newly established in v2, p.9-11 — for the overall assessment of a *multi-item* bid evaluation service, a separate track)
> The consolidated grade for the entire service bundling several individual project evaluations. **A different track from the 24-question review of individual reports** (the scale also differs).
- **Ⅰ. Consistency and professionalism of report quality** (Questions 1-3): Excellent 50 / Good 40 / Somewhat poor 30 / Poor 20
- **Ⅱ. Lessons-report quality and usability** (Questions 1-3): Excellent 20 / 16 / 12 / 8
- **Ⅲ. Appropriateness and feasibility of the feedback-task implementation plan** (Questions 1-3): Excellent 30 / 24 / 18 / 10
- **Special rule**: if even one individual project within the service is **D (Non-Pass), it is rated "Poor" in Ⅰ**. Overall assessment of at least 200 characters (excluding spaces), with 5 included items.

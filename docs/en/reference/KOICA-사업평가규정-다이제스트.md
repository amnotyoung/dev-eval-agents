> Source: **Project Evaluation Regulation (Regulation No. 536, amended 2025.2.25)** — extracted from the HWP original with kordoc.
> This regulation is the **higher-level regulatory basis** for KOICA evaluation under Article 16 of the "Framework Act on International Development Cooperation" and related provisions. Our system's evaluation criteria, types, quality review, and principles are all consistent with this regulation.

---

## Article 1 Purpose
**Learning knowledge and lessons through evaluation, and enhancing institutional accountability.**

## Article 5 Evaluation Principles (5)
1. **Partnership** — encouraging participation and joint evaluation by recipient and donor institutions
2. **Impartiality** — balanced, unbiased evaluation
3. **Objectivity** — distinguishing value judgments from facts
4. **Transparency** — stating objectives and methodology, and **distinguishing facts and conclusions from value judgments and recommendations**
5. **Credibility** — good faith and sincerity, a transparent process

## Article 6 Evaluation Criteria (7) — the regulatory basis for our 6 criteria
| Regulation criterion | Our system |
|----------|------------|
| Relevance | `dac-relevance-evaluator` |
| Effectiveness (including inclusion of vulnerable groups and equity) | `dac-effectiveness-evaluator` |
| Efficiency | `dac-efficiency-evaluator` |
| **Impact** (long-term, transformative effect) | (excluded from the Final Evaluation composite score; in ex-post/impact evaluation) |
| Sustainability | `dac-sustainability-evaluator` |
| Coherence | `dac-coherence-evaluator` |
| Cross-cutting issues (gender, environment, human rights) | (reflected in Effectiveness equity, etc.) |
- ②: **Evaluation criteria may be applied selectively, taking each project's characteristics into account.** (Adding Validity for CTS projects also falls within this discretion.)

## Article 7 Evaluation Types — the basis for our two modes
**By timing:** ex-ante evaluation / mid-term evaluation / **Final Evaluation** (degree of achievement of performance targets at the point of completion) / **ex-post evaluation** (deriving and sustaining long-term results after completion).
- **Final Evaluation** = our 6-criteria project-evaluation team (DAC 6 criteria → A–F).
- **Impact Evaluation** = a causal-effect-measuring evaluation under separate guidelines (led by the Evaluation Office) → `impact-evaluation-reviewer`. (Linked to the Article 7 evaluation-type framework + Appended Table 3 evaluability assessment.)
**By evaluator:** external / internal / joint.

## Article 19 Guarantee of Evaluation Independence
The independence and autonomy of the evaluator are guaranteed. That is, neither side may make improper demands such as forcing arbitrary alteration, deletion, or addition of evaluation content.
→ Consistent with our "human gate": AI produces the draft and a human finalizes it, without infringing on **evaluation independence**.

## Article 23 Assignment of Evaluation-Target Grades
After domestic and field surveys, results and limitations are systematically analyzed and an **evaluation grade is assigned** (applying the Final Evaluation Guidelines for Conducting Evaluation mutatis mutandis).
→ The basis for our A–F grade calculation.

## Articles 27·28 Evaluation Quality Review — the basis for our quality inspector
- **Evaluation Quality Review Committee**: external experts, an **independent evaluation panel pool**, with the panel composed of **3 members**. Members with a conflict of interest with the quality-review target are excluded.
- Using the **Appended Table 1 quality-review criteria**, grades **A·B·C·D** are assigned; the chair aggregates the results of the 3 members → determines and resolves the final quality grade and pass/fail.
- Principle of **blind review** (the evaluator's information is not disclosed).
→ `report-quality-inspector` follows exactly this structure (24 items / A–D / blind / panel average).

## Chapter 6 Evaluation Ethics (Articles 39–48)
Ethics compliance (Art. 39) / **ethical principles: independence** and others (Art. 40) / consideration for evaluation participants (Art. 41) / **guarantee of anonymity** (Art. 42) / minimizing negative effects (Art. 43) / **confidentiality and protection of personal information** (Art. 46) / prevention of sexual harassment and sexual violence (Art. 48).
→ Our agents' common rules (anonymization, personal information, independence).

## Appended Table 3 Evaluability Assessment Criteria/Checklist (newly established 2024.07.30)
Assessment of **suitability as an impact-evaluation target** → linked to the `impact-evaluation-reviewer`'s pre-screening of target suitability.
